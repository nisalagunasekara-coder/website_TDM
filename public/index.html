The Problem We're Solving

The Word Garden addresses the core educational and psychological gaps for dyslexic learners by creating a non-competitive, multiplayer environment that fosters confidence through adaptive personalization and safe social interaction.

The Problem

Many children with dyslexia struggle to learn effectively because most educational tools are competitive, not personalized, and do not support confidence or social interaction.

Why It Matters

This matters because without proper support, children with dyslexia can lose confidence, struggle in education, and face long-term challenges in communication, learning, and future opportunities.

Current Gaps

Current solutions lack personalization, collaborative learning, confidence-building features, and proactive feedback, making them less effective for children with dyslexia.

Our Solution

Our solution is a multiplayer, adaptive learning game that uses reward-based motivation, anonymous voice interaction, and proactive feedback to support confidence, engagement, and effective learning for children with dyslexia.

Reward-Based Motivation System

Encourages progress through achievements and positive feedback instead of competition, helping children feel motivated and confident without fear of failure.

Anonymous Voice Chat

Allows players to communicate in a safe virtual environment without visual pressure, improving social interaction and building communication confidence.

Adaptive Personalized Learning

Adjusts difficulty levels, hints, and feedback based on each child’s performance, ensuring a comfortable learning pace and better learning outcomes.

What Makes This Unique

  • Innovation Point 1: A non-competitive reward-based system that replaces leaderboards with achievements and positive reinforcement to reduce anxiety and build confidence in dyslexic learners.
  • Innovation Point 2: A multiplayer anonymous voice communication system that enables safe social interaction without visual pressure, helping children improve communication skills and teamwork.
  • Innovation Point 3: An adaptive learning engine with anticipatory multimodal feedback that adjusts difficulty and provides real-time visual, audio, and interactive guidance to prevent errors before they occur.

Working Prototype

See It In Action

We have developed a fully functional MVP of a multiplayer dyslexia learning game using Unreal Engine (PC) and a Flutter-based mobile app, which supports real-time collaborative word challenges, voice communication, adaptive difficulty, and reward-based progression with anticipatory multimodal feedback (visual, audio, and haptic cues) to guide learners before errors occur.

The system has been fully tested with 0+ dyslexic children in a controlled study, demonstrating over 0% task completion accuracy, high engagement, reduced cognitive load, and improved confidence compared to single-player learning tools, thereby validating the effectiveness of our multimodal multiplayer approach.

Commercialization & Future Potential

From research to real-world impact. A strategic roadmap for transforming this academic project into a sustainable product.

Target Market

Potential Customers

Children with dyslexia (ages 6–12), primary schools, special education centers, therapists, and government literacy programs.

Industry Relevance

Highly relevant due to global adoption of digital learning and the demand for inclusive tools that address social and cognitive needs.

Global EdTech market is valued at over USD 250 billion, with special education being a high-demand, growing niche.

Deployment Pathways
